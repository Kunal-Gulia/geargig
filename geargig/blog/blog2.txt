🌱 What Are Carbon Credits?
Carbon credits are permits that allow a company or individual to emit a certain amount of carbon dioxide or other greenhouse gases. One credit equals one tonne of CO₂ emissions. These credits can be traded in a market, encouraging emissions reductions where it is cheapest to do so.

Businesses that reduce emissions beyond legal limits can sell surplus credits.

Others can purchase credits to offset their own emissions.

This creates a market-driven incentive for sustainability.

🏭 Carbon Credits in the Manufacturing Sector
The manufacturing industry is one of the largest contributors to carbon emissions. However, it's also uniquely positioned to benefit from the carbon credit system.

🔧 Opportunities for Businesses:
Carbon Capture Technology: Invest in or adopt emission-reducing technologies to generate sellable carbon credits.

Process Optimization: Upgrade machinery and improve energy efficiency to lower emissions and claim credits.

Carbon Credit Trading: Establish carbon credit trading as a new revenue stream.

Sustainability Branding: Use carbon neutrality to boost brand value, especially in ESG-conscious markets.

🌾 Carbon Credits in Agriculture
Agriculture is both a contributor to and a solution for climate change. Practices like no-till farming, agroforestry, and methane reduction in livestock management can generate carbon credits.

🚜 Business Models for Farmers & Agri-entrepreneurs:
Soil Carbon Sequestration: Implement regenerative practices to lock carbon in soil and sell credits.

Agroforestry Projects: Tree-planting programs that enhance biodiversity and earn carbon offsets.

Manure Management: Invest in biogas and composting systems for carbon savings.
